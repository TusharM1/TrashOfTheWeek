Overview

Can you believe it? We are almost through the eight month of this Trash of the Week project! Usually, people wait until they reach some big milestone like one year to celebrate how consistent they have been, but every week I take these photos, I remind myself what it means to be consistent. But before we delve into the trash this week, lets remind ourselves why I do this.

The reason why I have been doing this project every week to be conscientious of the impact I have on the environment. As I have reiterated many times in previous Trash of the Week updates, the environment is an important part of everyone’s life. Treating it well and keeping in mind the impact you are having on it is the first step to improving the environment for everyone. What you may not see is the things I don’t throw out, the things I give away, the things I reuse and repurpose, the things that I repair and continue to use. Every single thing I document on this page is a reminder of the improvements we can make as a society to eliminate waste and decrease consumption.

The ideas of Ian Bogost come to mind when thinking about the goal of this project. Ontography, a term that for this context means the study of “the human response to the physical landscape” (36), is particularly relevant here when talking about environmental issues. Aside from the most important one being climate change, other environmental issues may not be as well known, such landfills and trash disposal issues. A quote from his essay, “an ontograph is a landfill, not a Japanese garden. It shows how much rather than how little exists simultaneously, suspended in the dense meanwhile of being”(59), sheds some light on how this entire project is an ontograph in its own way. Being a reflection of ourselves and our impact on the world, this project is a mirror of societies daily life. In a thousand years, the ontograph of a trash of the week project will indeed look very different, reflective of a different society with different values.

Analyzing the content of this week’s trash, it’s a real average of what the last several weeks have been. Here we have a little bit of everything. There’s nothing too out of the ordinary here and that means its been a bit of an uneventful week. It sure feels like it. But look at it this way, if every week were exciting, would any week really be?

The three main categories this week are: consumables, fragrances, and wrappers. Although these categories don’t really fit exactly, I think they are close enough of an association for the categorization to be warranted. Hopefully, you don’t think its too much of a stretch.

This week was almost a perfect snapshot of what the average American might consume in the course of a week. Everything from food and drink, to used containers and scraps, my trash had it all. One particular wrapper has an interesting story behind it… But first, let’s review this week’s trash!

Gallery

Consumables:

This category has everything to do with food and drink. The two things I consumed this week, an enchilada bowl and a cup of soda were very tasty but probably not that good for you. I did a bit of grocery shopping this week where I picked up a lot of food and drink, so I figured the receipt would fit well into this category.

Fragrance:

This is a bit of a looser categorization, but I think it still fits pretty well. I got this AirWick a few weeks ago and it just finished. It was a fall season one anyway, no big deal. My body wash just finished but during my grocery shopping, I also picked up some more body wash since I was running out. Lastly, I just ran through the last of my hand sanitizer, I kind of liked clinical, toxic smell from the unscented kind.

Wrappers:

In this category, I combined everything that was a package containing something else. The mangled bag was from a shopping trip the week prior that I forgot to throw out. The polaroid packaging was from a friend who left it here when she opened it to take a polaroid photo. The keychain packaging was from something I received from a website called Chess.com, it was a very cool keychain in the shape of a chess pawn.

Item of the week: Keychain packaging.

This was my favorite item from the week because I received this as a gift from Chess.com, it is a really nice keychain, and it came in this cute little packaging. I put it on my keychain, and it really makes it stand out and identify it as my own.
